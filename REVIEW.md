## kva-reviewer-seo

### Research notes

- **SERP winners are URL-templated city+service pages.** Across all six target SERPs, the page types that consistently rank are dedicated city+service URLs: O'Grady's `/ashburn-va-services/landscape-design/` (~2,100 words, H1 "Landscape Design in Ashburn VA"), Kane Landscapes `/landscape-design-ashburn-va/` (H1 "Landscape Design and Landscape Installation in Ashburn, Virginia"), Reston Pavers `/paver-patio-installation-leesburg-va` (~2,800 words, H1 "Get Paver Patio Installation in Leesburg, VA"), Independence Landscape `/locations/herndon-va/` (32 location pages), and Outdoor Lifestyles `/loudoun-county-services/hardscape-services/`. KVA ships one single-page site with no `<title>{Service} in {City}, VA</title>` surface area.
- **Title/H1 pattern that wins:** `{Service} in {City}, VA | {Brand}` for title, `{Service} in {City, VA}` for H1. Reston Pavers, Kane, O'Grady's, and Independence all use minor variants. KVA's homepage title is "KVA Landscaping — Sterling VA Lawn Care, Hardscape & Landscape Design" — brand-led, not query-led.
- **Map-pack vs organic split.** All six queries return a 3-pack of GBP results above organic. Competitors that rank both places (O'Grady's, Independence, BrightView) have (a) a verified GBP with the website URL set and (b) dedicated site URLs that match the GBP categories. KVA's GBP currently has `website: null` per `scrape-google.json:13` — a free, high-leverage fix that 2026 GBP guides call out as an entity-alignment signal (BrightLocal/MapRanks 2026).
- **Schema gap vs competitors.** Schema.org `LocalBusiness` recommends `image` as an array (Google rich-result preference), plus `serviceArea`, `knowsAbout`, `founder`, `foundingDate`, `slogan`, `paymentAccepted`, `currenciesAccepted`. Current JSON-LD at `components/site/JsonLd.tsx` ships a single `image` string, no `serviceArea` (uses non-standard `PostalCodeSpecification` items under `areaServed` instead of `GeoCircle`/`City`/`Place`), no `Service[]` with `areaServed` per service, and no `FAQPage`. Schema.org docs: https://schema.org/LandscapingBusiness, https://schema.org/LocalBusiness, https://schema.org/areaServed.
- **GBP cross-channel gaps.** `scrape-google.json` shows: no website link, 7 Google reviews (KVA also has 158 on Thumbtack but Google reviews are the primary ranking signal per BrightLocal 2026), owner has replied to only 3/7 reviews, photo count is 4 (vs 30+ typical for ranking landscapers). 2026 GBP guides flag posting frequency as a top-tier signal — KVA has no posts visible.
- **Service-area page matrix (warranted by SERP evidence).** Top-5 revenue cells based on KVA's actual service mix + scraped review keywords ("backyard paver patio", "walkways") + SERP ranking-page patterns:
  - `/services/paver-patios-leesburg-va` — Reston Pavers ranks #1 with ~2,800 words; Matt Tengs review mentions exact intent
  - `/services/landscape-design-ashburn-va` — 615 Houzz designers serve Ashburn; high-intent SERP
  - `/services/lawn-care-herndon-va` — LawnStarter, LawnGuru, Independence, Green Hill, Blades of Green all rank
  - `/services/hardscape-loudoun-county-va` — Outdoor Lifestyles, Landscape Concepts, Epling all rank with county-level pages
  - `/services/snow-removal-sterling-va` — BrightView, Raven Rock, English Farms, SMG all rank; KVA already does this
  - Plus the home / Sterling lawn care anchor and a `/locations/sterling-va` hub. Per BrightLocal SAP guidance, each must be unique, not templated.
- **Technical issues found in the current build.** `app/sitemap.ts` only contains the home URL (1 entry); `app/robots.ts` is correct but bare. The `areaServed` array in JSON-LD uses `@type: "PostalCodeSpecification"`, which is a non-standard schema construct — Google's structured-data validator will accept it but it's weaker than `GeoCircle` (geo + radius, which KVA already has lat/lng/10mi for) or `City[]` instances. The footer hard-codes only 5 cities into prose but the city list elsewhere has 6 (`Great Falls` missing from About body).

## kva-reviewer-conversion

### Research notes

- **Customer-language map (from Google reviews + brand brief):** Real phrases customers use are "backyard paver patio," "walkways," "re-level an existing walkway," "removed old sod and planted new grass seed," "plant trimming and mulch installation," "weeds removed and mulching done for a few gardens," and the trust phrase "great communication, quality work." The site's service-pick options currently read "Lawn care / maintenance" / "Mulch + beds + planting" / "Hardscape / patio / walkway" — close but not the words customers actually search/say (no mention of "re-level," "fresh sod," "weed/mulch garden cleanup," or "garden design" which the Google scrape lists as a keyword).
- **Benchmark takeaways:** 70% of home-service searches happen on mobile (LocaliQ 2025 Home Services benchmarks, https://localiq.com/blog/home-services-search-advertising-benchmarks/); responding within 60 sec lifts conversion by 391% and waiting 5 min drops qualification odds by 80% (Kixie / GreetNow 2024, https://greetnow.com/blog/speed-to-lead-statistics-2024); 78% of buyers go with the FIRST company to respond (same source). The brand brief documents KVA's actual Thumbtack-tracked response is ~1 hour, but the site only commits to "within one business day" — under-selling a real differentiator.
- **Form-field benchmark:** Each additional field cuts completion 5-10%; forms with >8 fields complete <10% (involve.me, Mandy Web Design 2025, https://www.involve.me/blog/form-best-practices). Single-column layouts complete 15.4% faster than multi-column (same source). KVA's form is single-column (good) but the top row pairs Name + Phone in two columns on desktop — minor friction.
- **Competitor form patterns:**
  - Virginia Landscaping LLC, NVA Landscaping, Southview Design, Level Green: ALL push to a dedicated contact/consultation page from the homepage rather than embedding a form on the home scroll. KVA's embedded inline form on the homepage is actually a conversion *advantage* over these competitors — preserve it.
  - NVA Landscaping uses a concrete differentiator badge ("Project Timeline Guarantee — $250/day past deadline"). Hunter Green, Southview Design and others rely on tenure ("Founded in 1978," "22 Years in Business," "10,000 projects"). KVA's "9 years / 165+ reviews / Top Pro 2025" is on par, but the Top Pro badge is not used as a click-out proof link (Thumbtack's Top Pro badge is independently verifiable — strong trust signal underused).
  - Southview Design shows phone number 5x on the homepage; KVA shows it 4x (Nav, Hero, Estimate section, Footer). On mobile only Estimate + Footer remain visible — see finding `conv-02`.
- **KVA funnel walkthrough (desktop 1440×900 + mobile 390×844 in Claude-in-Chrome):**
  - Hero on mobile: subhead and CTAs ARE above the fold (good). Trust strip line wraps to 4 lines on 390px — readable but cluttered.
  - **Header phone link is `display: none` on mobile** (verified via `getComputedStyle`: returns `"none"` at 390px because of `hidden sm:flex`). Mobile visitors see ZERO click-to-call until they scroll past Hero CTA or reach the form/footer. Header has no hamburger / nav drawer either — `hasMobileMenu: false`.
  - No sticky CTA bar on mobile. Mid-page (~Process, ~Reviews) users have to scroll back up or down 1+ full screen to hit a form or phone.
  - Form: 4 required (name, phone, address, services), 2 optional (email, message). Phone-format placeholder uses `(703) 555-0100` — friendly. No SLA, no "text-friendly" cue, no privacy line until *after* submit button.
  - Success message ("we got it. A senior crew lead will text or call you within one business day") is reassuring but commits to a slower SLA than reality.
  - Service pick option "Snow plowing (seasonal)" is currently shown even in May — see `conv-10`.

- **Axe accessibility scan (mcp__a11y__scan_page, WCAG 2.0/2.1/2.2 A + AA tags):** **0 violations**, 28 passes, 1 incomplete. KVA passes AA cleanly.
